Where it all started?

Malayalees were part of the Delaware social fabric since the 70's. Madhavan uncle, Susheela Aunty and Kurien uncle settled in Delaware 3 decades ago. And since the expansion of global economy there have been more and more Malayalees migrating to America and in tune to that more Malayalee families started settling in Delaware. Thanks to the friendly and welcoming social neighbourhood of Delaware and the establishment of many Banking sector jobs in the area, number of malayalees in Delaware has been always increasing. The need for a formal association to promote our culture and to interact with other malayalee families in Delaware was always a dream of many folks in and around Delaware. A first meet was held on January 2nd 2010 at Taste of India restaurant on Kirkwood highway. Jijo Joseph, Joe Paul, Glady helped organize the first meeting.. Below is the meeting minutes prepared by Jijo Joseph which laid the foundation for forming a Malayalee Association in Delaware and anointing it as 'DELMA'.

First of all, let me make apologies for being late in sending this email . Had some issues at work . “ aadyam ariprasnam pinne pothukaryam”. I hope all agree with me .. J
We had 19 families(~50 people) that showed up on Jan 2nd for the Malayalee get together and lunch. ( few names came up like delma – Delaware Malayalee association or delma_na etc .. we don’t want to go for MAD or KAD … J . Anyway we have time but feel free to suggest names

All are requested to send the information of their Malayalee contacts to Jose and or to myself to make a database and a Google or yahoo group which can be used for controlled communication.
