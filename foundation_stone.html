Where it all started?

Malayalees were part of the Delaware social fabric since the 70's. First incumbents of Malayalees started settling in Delaware 3 decades ago. And since the expansion of global economy there have been more and more Malayalees migrating to America and in tune to that more Malayalee families started settling in Delaware. Thanks to the friendly and welcoming social neighbourhood of Delaware and the establishment of many Banking sector jobs in the area, number of malayalees in Delaware has been always increasing. The need for a formal association to promote our culture and to interact with other malayalee families in Delaware was always a dream of many folks in and around Delaware. A first meet was held on January 2nd 2010 at Taste of India restaurant on Kirkwood highway which laid the foundation for forming a Malayalee Association in Delaware and anointing it as 'DELMA'. Since then Delma has grown leaps and bounds and has been an active organization bringing together Malayalees across the Delaware Valley.
